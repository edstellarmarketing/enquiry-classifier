ENQUIRY CLASSIFICATION RULES FOR SALES TEAM

Classification Categories:
There are three types of enquiries based on completeness of information.

1. NO-DETAILED ENQUIRY
Definition: Enquiries that lack sufficient information to process.
Criteria:
- Missing contact details (no email, phone, or name)
- Incomplete or no training information
- Vague or unclear requirements
- No specific training course mentioned
- Missing both contact information AND training details

2. SEMI-DETAILED ENQUIRY
Definition: Enquiries that have partial information but are missing key details.
Criteria:
- Has contact details (email, phone, or name) BUT no training information
- Has training information BUT missing contact details
- Has some details but not complete enough to process
- One aspect is detailed while the other is missing

3. DETAILED ENQUIRY
Definition: Complete enquiries ready for processing.
Criteria:
- Complete contact details (name, email, and/or phone number)
- Detailed training requirement information including:
  * Specific training course or topic
  * Number of participants
  * Preferred dates or timeframe
  * Training objectives or specific needs
- All necessary information to proceed with a proposal

Examples:

NO-DETAILED ENQUIRY Example:
"I want training for my team"
(No contact info, no specific training details)

SEMI-DETAILED ENQUIRY Example:
"Hi, I'm John Smith (john@company.com). I need training for my team."
(Has contact but no training details)

DETAILED ENQUIRY Example:
"Hello, I'm Sarah Johnson from ABC Corp (sarah@abccorp.com, +1-555-0123). We need Python programming training for 15 employees in January 2024. We want to focus on data analysis and automation skills."
(Complete contact and detailed training requirements)
